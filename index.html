Post 2 · Tanzania
🇹🇿 Tanzania: Geology and Reserves. Facts Without the Hype
East Africa's leading producer. ~50–60 tonnes annually. Real entry points for traders explained.
Read more ↓
  • Lake Victoria Goldfields — ~70% of industrial production. Grade: 3–5 g/t
  • Lake Tanganyika Goldfields — High-grade alluvial. ~1.5–2M artisanal miners
  • Central Dodoma Belt — Underexplored, new licences since 2022

Total resources: 80+ million ounces. Major mines (Barrick, AngloGold) closed to external buyers. Primary flow → ASM cooperatives, 400–800 kg/year per cooperative.

Tanzania = predictable geology + developed infrastructure + substantial ASM sector.
Post 3 · Tanzania
🇹🇿 Export Permits and the Doré Strategy
Why professionals choose doré 97–98% over 999.9%. Complete documentation and cost breakdown.
Read more ↓

Documentation required:

  • Export Permit (TMAA) — 5–7 days, $500–1,500
  • Assay Certificate — accredited laboratory
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Refinery Certificate (~1 kg bars after smelting)
  • Insurance — Brink's or Malca-Amit mandatory

Fees: Royalty 6% + Inspection 1% + Admin $200–400

Why doré wins: Speed (5–7 days to shipment), broad Dubai buyer pool, one final refining instead of two.
Post 4 · Tanzania
🇹🇿 Refineries and Export Preparation
Key refineries, capabilities, financial incentives. Choosing the right facility for doré bullion.
Read more ↓
  • MPMR Mwanza — Largest in East Africa, up to 480 kg/day. State-private (25% Stamico)
  • Geita Gold Refinery — Primary LBMA candidate. AngloGold Ashanti partnership
  • Eyes of Africa (EOA) — Flexible for small/medium batches, Bank of Tanzania program
Tax incentives: Royalty 4% (vs 6%) when selling to BoT. VAT 0% on gold to licensed refineries. 20% domestic refining mandate since July 2024.

Post 7 · Kenya
🇰🇪 Kenya: Licensing and Export Realities
Mineral Dealer's Licence — shortest path. Export steps, hidden costs, realistic timelines.
Read more ↓

Mineral Dealer's Licence: 1 year renewable, $5,000–8,000. 6–10 weeks processing (digitized since 2024).

Export steps:

  • Purchase from ASM/small miners with origin documentation
  • Local processing to 98%+ doré bars
  • Export Permit — 7–14 days, 3–5% royalty
  • Air freight Nairobi (NBO) → DXB/DOH
Hidden costs: Transport to refinery $1,000–2,000. Licensing + admin $3,000–5,000.
Post 8 · Uganda
🇺🇬 Uganda: Geology and Strategic Gold Status
Gold classified as strategic mineral in 2022. Unique dual export system — Model A vs Model B.
Read more ↓
  • Karamoja Region: ~90% of national output. High silver content (natural electrum) — valued by Dubai refiners
  • Buhweju District: Rich alluvials up to 10 g/m³, historic since 1920s

Dual Export System:

  • Model A (doré 92–98%): Negotiated prices, 0.5% export levy, pricing flexibility
  • Model B (999.9%): LBMA pricing reference, ministerial approval required
Note: AGR lost LBMA accreditation in 2022 (OFAC sanctions). Euro Gold Refinery launched 2025 as replacement.
Post 9 · Uganda
🇺🇬 Uganda: Licensing and Export Procedures
Trader-friendly framework. Official 2025 MDL fees, requirements, export steps, key advantages.
Read more ↓

Mineral Dealers Licence (MDL) — 2025:

  • Fee: UGX 10,000,000 (~$2,600) + application UGX 500,000
  • Requirements: financial resources proof, audited accounts, office premises, URA tax clearance

Export procedure:

  • Valid MDL + supplier documentation
  • Export Permit from DGSM (per consignment)
  • URA clearance and fee payment
  • Certificate of Origin + authenticated assay
Key advantage: Negotiated pricing for doré — no state price fixation. 3–5 working days processing.
Post 10 · DRC
🇨🇩 DRC: Geology and High-Risk/High-Reward Regions
~50% of global cobalt, significant gold. Region-by-region security assessment. Viable entry points only.
Read more ↓
  • Ituri Province: Government-controlled. 5–15 g/t, 95% native nuggets. Viable with proper security
  • North Kivu: Active conflict — NOT recommended for standard operations
  • Kibali Mine (Haut-Uélé): Isolated from Kivu conflict. Industrial mining possible
ICGLR RCM mandatory for legal export (not iTSCi — that applies to 3T minerals). Without certification: confiscation risk.
Post 11 · DRC
🇨🇩 DRC Gold Export: Compliance Reality Check
Most regulated process in Central Africa. Full cost breakdown: licences, certifications, taxes, logistics corridor.
Read more ↓

Per-shipment mandatory costs:

  • ICGLR RCM validation: $200–500
  • CEEC Certificate of Origin: 0.176–1.5% of value
  • Royalty: 3.5% + Export tax: ~2%

Logistics: Bukavu/Goma → Kigali → Dubai

  • Ground transport (Brink's/PSC): $800–1,500
  • Air freight (Qatar Airways): $4,000–6,000/50kg
  • Insurance (Lloyd's/AIG): 0.5–1% of value
Why DRC: High-grade doré (90–95%), competitive ASM prices, DRC Gold Refinery launched 2026.
